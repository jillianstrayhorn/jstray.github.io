Jillian C. Strayhorn, PhD

Assistant Professor, NYU School of Global Public Health
Associate Director, CADIO

I am an interdisciplinary scholar situated at the place where intervention science, experimental design, and decision science meet. I completed my BA in Psychology at Cornell University (2014), where I graduated summa cum laude and was recognized as a Merrill Presidential Scholar. I completed my PhD in Human Development and Family Studies at Penn State University (2022), under the mentorship of Linda Collins and David Vanness. There, I found some resolution to a longtime "identity crisis" in my own scholarly interests, which had always been torn between more applied disciplines (psychology) and more technical disciplines (math). For me, decision science represents the coolest blend of both, while intervention optimization offers among the most interesting of decision problems.

My dissertation work, which was honored with Penn State's Alumni Association Dissertation Award, laid the foundation for what became DAIVE, Decision Analysis for Intervention Value Efficiency, a framework that supports multi-criteria decision-making in intervention optimization. Rigorous optimization trials generate lots of rich empirical data (e.g., using factorial designs), and DAIVE facilitates the use of that data to decide which version of an intervention, from among many possible, should be considered "optimal." Often this means balancing competing criteria, including intervention effectiveness on different desired outcomes, and different resource use considerations, such as an intervention's cost to implement. I love that this work has given me the opportunity to collaborate with a wide variety of teams, working with a wide variety of interventions, tackling a wide variety of public health challenges, from smoking to interpersonal conflict to sugar consumption.
