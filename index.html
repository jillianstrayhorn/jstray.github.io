CADIO

The Center for Advancement and Dissemination of Intervention Optimization — championing the science of intervention optimization to engineer solutions for greater public health impact.

About us

CADIO is housed within the School of Global Public Health at New York University. Established by Dr. Linda M. Collins in 2021, our mission is to promote, catalyze, and support research involving intervention optimization.

Intervention optimization is an emerging scientific field. It integrates ideas from behavioral science, engineering, public health, quantitative and qualitative methods, economics, and decision science to produce innovative approaches for the empirical development and optimization of interventions — behavioral, biobehavioral, biomedical, social-structural, and educational.

How we think: MOST and EASE

Much of our work uses the Multiphase Optimization Strategy (MOST), an engineering-inspired framework for the development, optimization, and evaluation of interventions. The goal is to balance effectiveness against the real-world constraints on resources available for implementation.

We call this balance intervention EASE — a strategic balance of Effectiveness, Affordability, Scalability, and Efficiency.

Research focus

Researchers connected to CADIO apply intervention optimization across a wide range of domains, including:

  • Substance misuse, HIV/AIDS, and Hepatitis C
  • Smoking cessation and tobacco control
  • Cancer treatment, weight management, and nutrition
  • Mental health and well-being
  • Child maltreatment prevention and educational achievement

Partners & support

CADIO partners with the d3center at the University of Michigan. The center is supported by a NIDA P50 Research Center of Excellence grant (NIH P50DA054039).
